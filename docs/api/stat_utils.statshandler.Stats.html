compute_cr_energy_deposited()¶

Compute the total number of electrons deposited at each label.

This is simply a sum of all pixel values identified as belonging to a given cosmic ray.

  • \(I_0 = \sum_{i} p_i\)

Returns

cr_sum – Sum of all pixels at each label in label_ids

Return type

numpy.ndarray

compute_first_moment(sci=None)¶

Compute the first moment of energy deposited by a given cosmic ray.

This corresponds to the flux-weighted centroid of the cosmic ray denoted by \((I_x, I_y).\)

  • \(I_x = \frac{1}{I_0} \sum_{i}p_i * x_i\)

  • \(I_y = \frac{1}{I_0} \sum_{i}p_i * y_i\)

Parameters

sci (numpy.ndarray [sci]) – The sci attribute of the CosmicRayLabel object.

compute_higher_moments(energy_deposited, centroid, grid_coords, idx)¶

Compute all second moments of the distribution

  • \(I_{xx} = \frac{1}{I_0} \sum_{i}p_i(x_i - I_x)^2\)

  • \(I_{yy} = \frac{1}{I_0} \sum_{i}p_i(y_i - I_y)^2\)

  • \(I_{xy} = \frac{1}{I_0} \sum_{i}p_i(x_i - I_x)*(y_i - I_y)\)

Parameters

  • energy_deposited (float) – Total energy deposited by a single cosmic ray

  • centroid (tuple) – (x,y) position of the centroid of the cosmic ray

  • label_coords (tuple) – tuple of cosmic ray x coordinates and y coordinates

Returns

  • I_rr (tuple) – The second moments of the energy distribution in x and y, (\(I_{xx}\), \(I_{yy}\))

  • I_xy (int) – The cross moment of the energy distribution, \(I_{xy}\)

  • label_coords (list) – List of tuple’s containing the positions of each pixel hit by the cosmic ray

compute_shape(I_rr, I_xy)¶

Compute the “shape” of the distribution of the energy deposited

This is effectively a measure of symmetry of the distribution that is defined as follows:

\(shape = \sqrt{\frac{(I_{xx} - I_{yy})^2 + 4I^2_{xy}}{(I_{xx} + I_{yy})^2}}\)

Parameters

  • I_rr (tuple) – Second moment of energy distribution \((I_{xx}, I_{yy})\)

  • I_xy (int) – Cross moment of energy distribution

Returns

shape – The computed shape

Return type

int

compute_size(I_rr, label_coords)¶

Compute the size of the cosmic ray in two ways

  1. Compute the width or size of the cosmic energy distribution using the previously computed second moments.

    \(size = \sqrt{\frac{I_{xx} + I_{yy}}{2}}\)

  2. Compute the size of the cosmic ray in terms of the number of pixels that it affects.

Parameters

I_rr (tuple) – Second moment of energy distribution \((I_{xx}, I_{yy})\)

Returns

  • size_sigmas (float) – Width of the cosmic ray energy distribution

  • size_pixels (float) – Total number of pixels affected by the given cosmic ray
